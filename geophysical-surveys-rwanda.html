Rwanda's geology and the Kivu Rift

Rwanda sits on the western (Albertine) branch of the East African Rift. Its highlands are built from Precambrian basement — metasediments, gneisses and granites — cut by intrusive complexes and pegmatite belts, with rift sediments in the western lowlands around Lake Kivu. The terrain is deeply weathered and steep, so a proper subsurface investigation is essential before dams, roads, hospitals or mining works.

GeoResolve combines non-invasive geophysics with targeted drilling to reduce risk and cost. For dams we map the dam axis and abutments with seismic refraction and MASW (shear-wave velocity, Vs30), locate seepage and altered zones with ERT, and confirm bedrock with boreholes and SPT. For buildings we deliver seismic site classification; for mining we deploy magnetic, EM and ERT over the pegmatite belts.

Methods we offer in Rwanda

  • Seismic refraction & tomography — bedrock depth, rippability and foundation characterisation.
  • MASW — shear-wave velocity profiling and seismic site classification (Vs30).
  • Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT) — 2D/3D imaging of foundations, groundwater and mineralised zones.
  • Magnetic & EM surveys — structural and mineral-exploration mapping.
  • Groundwater (VES/ERT) — borehole siting and aquifer assessment.
  • Drilling & SPT — boreholes, in-situ testing and material sampling.
