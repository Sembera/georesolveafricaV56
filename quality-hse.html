Quality Assurance

Our data is only as good as the chain of custody behind it. Georesolve applies a field data quality control (QC) workflow across the full project lifecycle so that the numbers we hand to designers can be trusted and defended:

  • Calibration checks before mobilisation. Every geophysical instrument, drilling rig sensor and survey GNSS unit is checked and calibrated against schedule before it leaves the depot, and field-checked again on arrival at site.
  • Daily QC review of field records. Each day's survey data and borehole observations are reviewed while the crew is still on site, so gaps, spikes or setup errors are caught and re-collected before demobilisation.
  • Logged-by / checked-by sign-off. Every borehole log and every survey record carries a logged-by and a checked-by sign-off, so responsibility for the data is explicit and traceable from field sheet to final report.
  • Standards we follow. Our investigations are aligned with recognised methods and reporting standards, including BS 5930 (site investigations), ISO 14688 / ISO 14689 (geotechnical and geogical descriptions), ASTM test methods, and ISRM suggested methods for rock testing and characterisation.
  • Instrument calibration schedule. Calibration intervals are logged per asset, and out-of-tolerance equipment is withdrawn from service until it is verified.
  • Independent review before reporting. Interpretations, models and conclusions are peer-reviewed by a second geoscientist before issue; raw data and processing steps are archived so any result can be reconstructed and audited.

Where a project specification calls for a particular standard or code, we adopt it and record conformance — our procedures are aligned with the recognised references above rather than assumed to meet them by default.

Health & Safety

Field geoscience carries real hazards — traffic, drilling, steep terrain, water and remote access. We manage them proactively, before anyone steps on site:

  • Pre-mobilisation risk assessments. A site-specific risk assessment and method statement are completed and signed off before crews mobilise, covering access, terrain, plant and local hazards.
  • Daily toolbox talks. Every shift opens with a short briefing on the day's tasks, hazards and controls, so the whole crew starts aligned.
  • PPE requirements. Personal protective equipment is issued and enforced per task and per client site rules — head, eye, ear, foot, hi-vis and task-specific protection as needed.
  • Journey management for remote sites. Travel to and from remote or cross-border locations follows a journey management plan with agreed routes, check-in times and communication arrangements.
  • Community liaison before entering survey areas. Before surveying land, we engage local leaders and landowners, explain the work, and agree access and timing to avoid conflict and disruption.
  • Incident reporting. Every incident and near-miss is reported, recorded and reviewed, and the lesson is fed back into our procedures. Anyone can stop an unsafe task without blame.

Our goal is a record of zero harm — to our people, to contractors, and to the public near our worksites.

Environmental Responsibility

Non-invasive geophysics is inherently low-impact, and we keep it that way by choosing the lightest method that answers the question:

  • Minimal-impact, non-invasive methods first. Seismic, electrical, magnetic, GPR and InSAR methods characterise the subsurface without excavation wherever the investigation allows.
  • Full site reinstatement after trenching or drilling. Where pitting, trenching or drilling is required, the site is made safe and reinstated on demobilisation — backfill, compaction and clearing of all temporary works.
  • Waste management. Drilling fluids, fuels, lubricants and samples are handled and stored to prevent soil or water contamination, and removed for proper disposal.
  • Respect for cultural and protected sites. We identify and avoid protected areas, watercourses, cultural heritage sites and community land, and obtain the right permissions before any ground disturbance.
