The Kivu Rift and Virunga geology

Eastern DRC lies along the western (Albertine) branch of the East African Rift. Around Goma and Bukavu the ground is built from young alkaline volcanics of the Virunga chain, interbedded rift sediments and deeply weathered basement — a complex, variable setting where a proper subsurface investigation is essential before mining, dam or infrastructure works. Further north, Ituri sits on Archean basement and greenstone belts that host significant gold and pegmatite mineralisation.

GeoResolve combines non-invasive geophysics with targeted drilling to reduce risk and cost. For mining we deploy magnetic, EM, IP and ERT to locate and prioritise targets; for infrastructure and dams we use seismic refraction, MASW and ERT to map foundations, slopes and groundwater; and for communities we site boreholes with VES/ERT.

Methods we offer in the DRC

  • Seismic refraction & MASW — bedrock depth, rippability and Vs30 site classification.
  • Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT) — foundation, groundwater and mineralised-zone imaging.
  • Magnetic, EM & IP — mineral-exploration and structural mapping.
  • Groundwater (VES/ERT) — borehole siting and aquifer assessment.
  • Drilling & SPT — boreholes, in-situ testing and sampling.
  • Geological mapping & pitting/trenching — outcrop to target support.
